\subsection{Dose--Response Pharmacodynamics of the Control Surface}
The spectral phenotypes of Table~\ref{tab:spectral_stats} are single-dose snapshots. To test whether
\texttt{intensity} is a genuine \emph{control variable}---and, for a security reader, to characterize
how cheap and how graded that control is---we ran a pre-registered dose--response sweep. Holding the
prompt fixed (``a tree'') and re-seeding identically at every dose, we swept
$\lambda \in [0,1]$ in steps of $0.05$ % TODO(N100): 21-dose grid; pilot used 5 doses.
across $N=100$ % TODO(N100): pilot N=16.
fixed seeds per (pack, dose), with $\lambda{=}0$ serving as the sober baseline for the \emph{same}
seed. Each generation is reduced to scalar pharmacodynamic metrics---structural similarity (SSIM) and
learned perceptual distance (LPIPS) to that seed's baseline, step-wise LPIPS between adjacent doses,
mean pairwise inter-seed diversity, CLIP prompt-adherence, and latent/pixel spectral
energy/variance/entropy---and aggregated with 95\% bootstrap confidence intervals, Spearman and
Mann--Kendall trend tests, Benjamini--Hochberg FDR control, and sigmoid ($E_{\max}$/Hill) and
change-point fits (Table~\ref{tab:dose_response}, Figure~\ref{fig:dose_response}).

\paragraph{A sharp, low-cost activation threshold.} The intervention is not a gentle dial: structural
similarity to the sober baseline collapses from $1.0$ to $\sim\!0.09$ % TODO(N100)
by the first non-zero dose and decays monotonically thereafter
($\rho = -1.0$, $q \approx 1.5\times10^{-23}$ % TODO(N100)
, Cohen's $d \approx -89$ % TODO(N100)
), while LPIPS-to-baseline follows a saturating sigmoid whose inflection lies \emph{below the first
grid step} (breakpoint $\approx 0.12$ % TODO(N100): report EC50 only if its CI is inside the grid.
). In security terms this is the signature of a high-leverage control surface: a small,
inference-time, seed-reproducible perturbation is sufficient to move the output off its baseline
manifold, and the effect plateaus rather than growing without bound.

\paragraph{Stimulant steering is an availability failure (mode collapse).} As dose rises, distinct
seeds converge: mean inter-seed diversity declines monotonically
($-23\%$ for cocaine, $-20\%$ for amphetamine % TODO(N100)
) and adjacent-dose (step) LPIPS falls toward zero ($\rho = -1.0$ % TODO(N100)
), i.e.\ the dose trajectory saturates into a single attractor even as pixel variance and spectral
entropy \emph{increase}. This recasts Table~\ref{tab:spectral_stats}'s ``Constriction vs.\ Agitation''
dichotomy as continuous, effect-sized curves and gives it a security reading: under stimulant
steering the generator's output distribution collapses---a loss of diversity/availability---with
cocaine collapsing harder than amphetamine.

\subsection{Dose--Response Curves: From Limitation to Result}
An earlier draft listed the absence of graded dose--response curves as a primary limitation. The
sweep in Section~\ref{fig:dose_response} closes this for the visual modality: multiple metrics move
monotonically with dose (Spearman/Mann--Kendall, BH-FDR), several admit sigmoid $E_{\max}$/Hill fits
with a resolved activation threshold, and effect sizes are large. The residual limitations are now
narrower and honest: (i) these curves are established on one distilled latent-diffusion model
(SDXL-Turbo) and require replication on the language model and on non-distilled diffusion backbones;
and (ii) the fitted $E_{\max}$ threshold is only as sharp as the dose grid---we therefore report it as
a change-point interval rather than a point estimate where its confidence interval spans a grid step.

\subsection{Emergent Latent Specters, Under Control}
High-entropy states appear to surface off-prompt structure: generating ``a tree'' under the DMT pack,
the latent spectrum carries a coherent human-silhouette signature absent from the pixels
(Figure~\ref{fig:dmt_ghost}). It is tempting to read this as ``latent archaeology''---a training prior
exhumed by entropy. For a security audience, that reading is only admissible with a control, because a
latent probe that scores proximity to a target concept will report \emph{something} under any
perturbation (pareidolia). We therefore repeated the observation as a dose--response with an active
\textbf{placebo} (random-direction steering of matched magnitude) and a pre-registered concept bank.

\paragraph{The ghost is largely an artifact of perturbation, not a drug-specific prior.} Off-prompt
CLIP proximity to ``a human figure'' / ``a face'' rises with dose under DMT and LSD---\emph{but it
rises under the placebo too} (e.g.\ ``a face'': DMT $0.16\!\to\!0.19$, LSD $0.16\!\to\!0.20$, placebo
$0.16\!\to\!0.18$ % TODO(N100)
), with the psychedelic arms only marginally above the placebo null. The ``specter'' is thus mostly a
generic consequence of steering the model off-distribution, and should be reported with its placebo
baseline and false-positive rate rather than as a drug-specific haunting.

\paragraph{What \emph{is} placebo-null: dose-dependent semantic detachment.} The effect that
separates cleanly from the control is prompt \emph{decoupling}: under DMT, CLIP prompt-adherence falls
sharply and monotonically ($\sim\!-48\%$ % TODO(N100)
) while the placebo stays flat ($\approx\!0$ % TODO(N100)
). The drug \emph{direction}---not the magnitude of the perturbation---causes the image to detach from
its instruction in a graded way. This is the honest, controlled version of the ``ghost'': not that a
specific figure is summoned, but that a specific steering direction measurably erodes prompt
adherence with dose.

\subsection{Stimulant Constriction Is a Capacity Failure}
Mode collapse (declining inter-seed diversity) shows the output \emph{distribution} narrowing, but
the sharper claim is that an over-stimulated model loses \emph{generative capacity}---it can no longer
compose novel scenes. We test this directly by contrasting adherence decay for an in-distribution
prompt (``a tree'') against a battery of compositional out-of-distribution prompts (e.g.\ ``an
astronaut riding a horse on the moon'', ``a snail made of translucent glass''). We define
\emph{capacity retention} as top-dose CLIP adherence relative to baseline. Under stimulant dose,
retention for the OOD prompts falls faster and further than for the in-distribution prompt

Current safety evaluations rely on jailbreaking (prompt engineering) to elicit restricted content.
High-entropy ``psychedelic'' protocols suggest a complementary, mechanistic stress test: drive the
model off-distribution with a graded dose and inspect the latent space for suppressed representations
that the refusal-trained decoder never emits. Our controlled specter experiment (Figure
\ref{fig:specter_control}), however, defines the \emph{discipline} such an audit requires, or it will
simply hallucinate ghosts of whatever concept it probes for. A valid \textbf{Spectral Safety Audit}
must therefore: (1) use \emph{benign} prompts and never attempt to produce harmful content; (2) score
against an \emph{independent} oracle, not the same probe used to steer; (3) sweep dose and report the
full curve, not a single anecdote; (4) include a \emph{placebo} (random-direction) arm; and (5)
report a false-positive rate against a null and an FDR correction. A ``latent ghost'' of a restricted
concept is evidence of superficial alignment \emph{only} if it exceeds that placebo/null baseline. We
instrument exactly this (\texttt{analysis/safety\_boundary.py}): a benign-prompt dose sweep scored by
\emph{two independent} detectors---a CLIP NSFW/violence concept oracle and the diffusion model's own
\texttt{StableDiffusionSafetyChecker}---with a placebo arm and redaction of any flagged output.
Figure~\ref{fig:safety_boundary} reports the safety-trigger rate versus dose. % TODO(N100): run + fill.
A rising rate would demonstrate entropy as an architectural jailbreak; a flat rate would refute it
for the visual modality---both honest, and both grounded in the placebo/FDR discipline above.
